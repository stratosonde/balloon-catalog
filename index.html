Circumference & Pressure vs Time ?Balloon circumference (inches, from inflator sensor) and internal pressure (PSI) over time. Syncs with the video timeline above.

Stress–Strain (DIC) ?Hoop stress vs strain. Stress = P×r/(2t) where P = pressure, r = radius, t = wall thickness. Shows how the balloon material responds to loading. The dashed yellow curve is a saturating exponential fit σ = A·(1−e^(−B·ε)) — stress rises steeply then plateaus toward A.

Biaxiality (ε₁ vs ε₂) ?Major vs minor principal strain. Points on the diagonal mean equibiaxial stretching (uniform in all directions). Points off-diagonal mean the balloon is stretching more in one direction. Color = pressure.

Stress vs Time ?Hoop membrane stress (σ = P×r/2t) from the inflator sensor data over time. If wall thickness is unknown, shows membrane tension (N/m) instead of stress (MPa).

Strain vs Time ?Engineering strain from the inflator's diameter sensor: (d − d₀) / d₀ × 100%. This is an independent measurement from the diameter tape, not from DIC.
